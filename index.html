70s

The 1970s were marked by a diverse range of styles, reflecting the social and cultural changes of the era.

Disco: The disco era brought vibrant, flashy clothing with lots of glitter, sequins, and metallic fabrics. Platform shoes and bell-bottom pants were iconic.

Hippie Influence: The early 70s continued the hippie fashion from the late 60s, featuring tie-dye shirts, peasant blouses, maxi skirts, and denim jeans.

80s

The 1980s fashion was characterized by bold, extravagant styles and a strong sense of individuality.

Power Dressing: Business wear for women became popular, with padded shoulders, tailored suits, and pencil skirts. This look aimed to project confidence and authority.

Athleisure: The fitness craze of the 80s brought athleisure into everyday fashion. Leg warmers, spandex, sweatbands, and colorful tracksuits were ubiquitous.

90s

The 1990s saw a mix of minimalism and grunge, influenced by the cultural shifts of the decade.

Grunge: Grunge fashion, inspired by bands like Nirvana, included flannel shirts, ripped jeans, combat boots, and layered clothing. This style was characterized by a laid-back, unkempt look.

Minimalism: Contrasting with grunge, minimalism became a trend with simple, clean lines, and neutral colors. Slip dresses, high-waisted pants, and basic T-shirts were popular.
